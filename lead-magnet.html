01

Diaphragmatic Breathing

All Trimesters

Why It Matters

This is the foundation of everything. Proper breathing activates your deep core, manages pressure on your pelvic floor, and gives your baby more room. If you do nothing else from this guide, do this.

How to Do It

  1. Sit comfortably or lie on your side
  2. Place one hand on your chest, one on your belly
  3. Inhale through your nose — feel your belly and ribs expand outward (not up)
  4. Exhale through your mouth — feel your belly gently draw in
  5. The hand on your chest should barely move

Reps

10 slow breaths

Frequency

2-3 times per day

Tip

Practice this during stressful moments too. It activates your parasympathetic nervous system — calming for you and baby.

02

Cat-Cow Stretch

All Trimesters

Why It Matters

Relieves back tension, improves spinal mobility, and helps baby find a good position. As your belly grows, your lower back takes on more load — this counteracts it.

How to Do It

  1. Start on all fours — hands under shoulders, knees under hips
  2. Inhale: gently arch your back, lifting your tailbone and chest (cow)
  3. Exhale: round your spine, tucking your chin and tailbone (cat)
  4. Move slowly — let your breath guide the pace

Reps

8-10 rounds

Frequency

Daily

Third Trimester Tip

Make the movements smaller and focus on the exhale/round portion. This helps create space for baby.

03

Glute Bridges

All Trimesters — Modified in T3

Why It Matters

Your glutes are the powerhouse of your pelvis. Strong glutes support your lower back, stabilize your hips, and help during labor. Most pregnant women underuse their glutes — this fixes that.

How to Do It

  1. Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat on the floor hip-width apart
  2. Exhale and press through your heels to lift your hips
  3. Squeeze your glutes at the top — hold for 2 seconds
  4. Inhale and slowly lower

Reps

12-15 reps, 2 sets

Frequency

3-4x per week

After 20 Weeks

Avoid lying flat on your back for extended periods. Prop your upper back on a pillow or couch cushion so your heart is above your hips.

04

Supported Squat Hold

All Trimesters

Why It Matters

Squatting opens your pelvis, strengthens your legs, and is one of the most functional positions for labor. The supported version keeps you safe and stable as your center of gravity shifts.

How to Do It

  1. Stand with feet slightly wider than hip-width, toes turned out slightly
  2. Hold onto a doorframe, countertop, or sturdy chair for support
  3. Inhale and lower into a squat — as deep as comfortable
  4. Exhale and hold for 5-10 seconds
  5. Press through your heels to stand

Reps

8-10 reps

Frequency

3-4x per week

Tip

If deep squatting is uncomfortable, only go to where it feels good. Depth improves over time.

05

Side-Lying Hip Abduction

All Trimesters

Why It Matters

Stabilizes your pelvis, strengthens your outer hips, and helps prevent the hip pain that many pregnant women experience in the second and third trimesters.

How to Do It

  1. Lie on your side, bottom knee bent for stability, top leg straight
  2. Exhale and lift your top leg to hip height — keep your hips stacked (don't roll back)
  3. Hold for 2 seconds at the top
  4. Inhale and slowly lower

Reps

12-15 each side, 2 sets

Frequency

3-4x per week

Tip

If this feels easy, add a light resistance band around your thighs for extra challenge.
